# Grounding Techniques

Grounding techniques help bring your focus to the present moment and can be especially helpful during times of anxiety, dissociation, or overwhelming emotions.

## The 5-4-3-2-1 Technique

This sensory awareness exercise helps anchor you to the present:

1. **5 things you can SEE**: Look around and name five things you can see
2. **4 things you can TOUCH**: Notice four things you can physically feel
3. **3 things you can HEAR**: Listen for three sounds in your environment
4. **2 things you can SMELL**: Identify two scents around you
5. **1 thing you can TASTE**: Notice one taste in your mouth

## Physical Grounding

### Body-Based Techniques
- **Feet on floor**: Press your feet firmly into the ground
- **Cold water**: Splash cold water on your face or hold ice
- **Deep breathing**: Slow, deep breaths from your diaphragm
- **Progressive muscle relaxation**: Tense and release muscle groups

### Movement-Based Techniques
- Walking slowly and mindfully
- Stretching
- Yoga poses
- Dancing or shaking

## Mental Grounding

### Cognitive Techniques
- **Categories game**: Name items in a category (colors, animals, cities)
- **Math exercises**: Count backwards from 100 by 7s
- **Describe your surroundings**: Narrate what you see in detail
- **Anchoring phrases**: Repeat calming statements

## When to Use Grounding

Grounding is particularly helpful when experiencing:
- Flashbacks or intrusive memories
- Dissociation or feeling disconnected
- Panic attacks
- Overwhelming anxiety
- Strong emotional reactions

## Creating a Grounding Kit

Consider assembling items that engage your senses:
- Textured objects (stress ball, fabric)
- Essential oils or scented items
- Photos of safe places or loved ones
- Comforting music playlist
- Sour candy or strong mints
